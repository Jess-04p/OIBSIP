Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam(1931-2015)

- the 'Missile Man of India'



Introduction

Dr APJ Kalam , also known as Abul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam , was born on 15th October 1931 in Rameshwaram , Tamil Nadu . He was called the ‘ missile man ‘ of India . He was a famous scientist of the country . He became the eleventh President of India . He served the country from 2002 to 2007. Dr Kalam became the Scientific Advisor to Prime Minister due to his hard work and intelligence. He was a man with a vision.


Early Life and Education

He belongs to a very poor family and he started working at an early age to support his family. After the school hours, Dr Kalam distributed newspapers to support his father Jainulabdeen, who was a boat owner. He completed his school education from Schwartz higher secondary school at Ramanathapuram. After that , he studied science and completed his B.Sc. from Saint Joseph College in Tiruchirappalli , Tamil Nadu . His main subject was Physics. Then he moved to Madras in 1955 to study aerospace engineering from Madras Institute of Technology .


Kalam as a Scientist

Dr Kalam joined the Defense Research and Development Organization ( DRDO ) as a scientist and started his career by designing a small helicopter for the Indian Army . For his excellence and brilliance, he was awarded the prestigious Bharat Ratna in 1998; Padma Vibhushan in 1990 ; and Padma Bhushan in 1981 .


Conclusion

Dr Kalam was known for his motivational speeches and interaction with the student community . He was fond of music and loved reading the Quran and the Gita . He was a genius and a man full of energy , spirit and vision . The legend and the great veteran took his last breath in Shillong while delivering a lecture to the students of IIM on 27th July , 2015 .
